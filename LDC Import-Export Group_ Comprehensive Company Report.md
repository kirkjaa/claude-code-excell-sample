# LDC Import-Export Group: Comprehensive Company Report

## 1. Executive Summary

LDC Import-Export Group, established in 2011, is a diversified business conglomerate based in Laos PDR. The group has grown significantly from its initial focus on importing and distributing products under the U-Express brand to operating a wide array of subsidiaries across retail, food services, and specialized equipment distribution. With a strong commitment to national development, LDC Group aims to be a leading company in Laos, emphasizing quality service, diverse product offerings, strategic partnerships, and adherence to high standards. The company employs over 400 staff members and operates 33 retail branches across various provinces in Laos [1].

## 2. Company Overview

**Establishment and Growth:**
Founded in 2011, LDC Import-Export Group began its operations by providing services and distributing both domestic and imported products in Vientiane Capital. The initial U-Express brand was inspired by the French retail company Système U [1]. A pivotal moment in the company's growth was the opening of its first Jiffy convenience store in 2014 at a PTT gas station in Laos PDR. The Jiffy brand quickly gained consumer trust and popularity, leading to further expansion [1].

**Vision and Mission:**
LDC Group's overarching vision is to establish itself as a leading company within Laos, characterized by a modern and comprehensive retail business model, excellent standards, and superior service. This vision is underpinned by a core mission focused on four key areas [1]:

*   **Service:** Delivering standardized and prompt service to cultivate customer confidence.
*   **Products:** Providing a broad selection of high-quality, cost-effective products that cater to customer needs.
*   **Partnerships:** Cultivating unity and fostering strong relationships both internally within the organization and externally with business partners.
*   **Standards:** Building trust with customers and employees while maintaining a strong commitment to societal and environmental well-being.

**Organizational Structure and Workforce:**
The group operates with a systematic management structure that oversees all its subsidiaries, with a strategic focus on sustainable growth. LDC Group is a significant employer in Laos, with a workforce exceeding 400 individuals [1]. Its retail and distribution network comprises 33 branches strategically located in community areas and at gas stations across key provinces, including Vientiane Capital, Luang Prabang, Khamwon, Sabanakhet, Pakse, and Atapu. The company also boasts a robust distribution center and over 10 support departments, encompassing critical functions such as Human Resources and Training, IT, Accounting and Finance, Marketing, Procurement, Packaging, Warehousing and Sorting, Audit, and Business Analysis [1].

## 3. Subsidiaries and Operations

LDC Group has strategically diversified its business interests through several key subsidiaries, each catering to specific market segments:

### 3.1. Jiffy Convenience Store

Jiffy is a highly recognized convenience store brand under the LDC Group, known for its widespread presence and consumer trust in Laos. The brand was introduced to the Lao market in 2014, building on a concept that originated in the United States and was expanded in Thailand by PTT Retail Management Co. [1] [7]. Jiffy stores offer a wide array of value-for-money products and are conveniently located at PTT gas stations and within local communities. As of early 2018, there were 21 Jiffy shops, with the video transcription indicating a current total of 33 branches across various provinces [1].

### 3.2. Zaap by B Bakery and Meal to Go

Established in 2016, Zaap by B Bakery and Meal to Go specializes in producing ready-to-eat fresh meals, baked goods, and desserts. These products are distributed through Jiffy and SkyMart stores nationwide. The brand emphasizes quality, convenience, and freshness, offering a diverse menu that includes Japanese cuisine, European-style dishes, and local specialties to cater to time-constrained consumers [1].

### 3.3. SkyCool Import-Export Co., Ltd.

SkyCool, organized in 2017, serves as the authorized distributor for Daikin, the world's leading air-conditioning brand, in Laos [1]. The company aims to be a central distributor for a range of world-class electric and air conditioning brands, including Schneider Electric, Philips Lighting, Pyramid Autodoor, Air Filtration Solutions AAF, Cytec Precision Air Conditioning Systems, Carrier Air Conditioners, and Samsung [1]. SkyCool operates a showroom, a comprehensive service center, and a training center in Vientiane Capital to ensure adherence to high sales and service standards. The company is supported by over 100 authorized dealers across Laos and provides extensive after-sales services, including maintenance contracts, repair services, parts sales, and overhaul/replacement options [1] [4] [5].

### 3.4. CR Service

CR Service focuses on providing essential equipment and maintenance services primarily to the business-to-business (B2B) sector, including minimarts, hotels, and restaurants. Their offerings encompass a wide range of products such as air conditioners, refrigerators, cold rooms, shelving, ice machines, CCTV cameras, microwaves, and coffee machines, along with comprehensive maintenance and installation services [1].

### 3.5. Gourmet

Gourmet operates as both a restaurant and a supplier of ingredients for general customers, hotels, and restaurants. Its product portfolio is extensive, featuring bakery items, beverage ingredients, wine, dry goods, seasonings, and imported meat and seafood. Gourmet has also ventured into e-commerce, providing a platform for product sales and delivery services [1].

### 3.6. Ants E-Commerce

Ants E-Commerce is a newly established venture by LDC Group, currently in its preparatory phase for launch [1]. While specific details about its offerings are emerging, available information suggests a comprehensive e-commerce ecosystem. This includes a consumer-facing application (E-Ants for shopping), a merchant application (Ants Merchant for store management), and a rider application (Ants Rider for delivery services), all developed by IQR, indicating a robust digital strategy [6]. The launch of Ants E-Commerce aligns with the broader growth of the e-commerce sector in Laos, supported by new electronic registration systems and platforms [2] [3].

## 4. Strategic Partnerships

LDC Group's success is significantly bolstered by its strategic partnerships with international and regional entities:

*   **PTT (Thailand):** A crucial partnership for the expansion of Jiffy convenience stores in Laos, with PTT Retail Management Co. holding the master franchise [1] [7].
*   **Daikin (Japan/Malaysia):** SkyCool's role as the authorized Daikin distributor highlights a strong collaboration in the air conditioning market, supported by Daikin Malaysia (DAMA) [1] [4].
*   **Schneider Electric:** LDC Group has a partnership with Schneider Electric to bring energy and digital solutions to Laos, positioning itself as a key partner for global brands [1].
*   **Système U (France):** The initial inspiration for the U-Express brand, demonstrating LDC Group's early engagement with international retail concepts [1].

## 5. Market Position and Future Outlook

LDC Group holds a significant position in the Lao retail and distribution landscape, driven by its diverse portfolio and strategic partnerships. The company's extensive network of Jiffy stores and its role as a distributor for major international brands like Daikin and Schneider Electric underscore its market influence. The expansion into fresh food production with Zaap and the nascent Ants E-Commerce platform indicate a forward-looking strategy aimed at capturing new market opportunities and adapting to evolving consumer behaviors, particularly in the digital space [1] [6].

The Lao economy, characterized by strong growth, presents a favorable environment for LDC Group's continued expansion [1]. The increasing buying power of the Lao population and improved access to quality goods and services further support the group's retail and distribution activities. The company's focus on creating employment and establishing robust distribution networks aligns with national development goals, contributing to its sustainable growth trajectory [1].
